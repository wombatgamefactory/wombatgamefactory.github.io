Bucket List

In Development

Collect the world, one card at a time.

Players 2–5
Play Time 30 Min
Age 8+
Weight 1.5 / 5
Status Seeking Publisher

Bucket List is a card game series built around the universal fantasy of collecting experiences — the places you've visited, the wonders you've seen, the creatures you've spotted in the wild. Each edition centres on a specific destination or theme, from the landmarks of London to the birds of the Australian bush, giving the series natural expandability and instant shelf appeal in tourist shops, nature centres, and gift stores worldwide. On every card is a small checkbox — because this is a game you keep playing long after the box is back on the shelf, ticking off each bird you've spotted or landmark you've finally stood in front of.

The core mechanism is clean and immediate: players draft cards from a shared market and place them into a growing personal tableau, building outward with each turn. Cards only need to connect to something already in play, so the tableau sprawls organically — but scoring rewards focus. Line up four cards in a row or column that share a matching symbol, and you score a point. Extend that run to five or six, and bonus points follow. That single constraint turns a casual drafting game into a satisfying spatial puzzle.

What makes Bucket List genuinely distinctive is how the theme earns its place. The symbols players are matching — habitat and diet for the birds edition, neighbourhood and activity type for London — aren't abstract icons bolted onto a generic mechanism. They reflect something real about the subject matter, giving players a reason to actually read the cards and absorb the facts. Combined with the checkbox, the game builds a quiet personal record: a growing list of things seen, visited, and still to come.

Bucket List appeals to families, couples, and groups of friends who want something light but clever — players who care more about the experience around the table than mastering a complex ruleset. The minimal component count and clean visual design keep manufacturing costs low, while the series format gives a publisher a proven template to roll out across cities, wildlife themes, and natural wonders with minimal redesign. It's a game you buy as a souvenir and play for years.
